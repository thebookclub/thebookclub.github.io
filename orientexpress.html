Murder on the Orient Express

By Agatha Christie

The most celebrated piece of work by Agatha Christie. Now a major motion picture.

A classic detective novel about a murder committed in the middle of the night on a train traveling from the Far East to Europe.

While the train is stuck in a snowdrift, the identities, motives and alibis of the passengers are investigated by the renowned Belgian detective Hercule Poirot.

As Poirot delves into the mystery of what happened that fateful night, the book explores themes relating to the nature of justice, the power of family ties, and the inevitable triumph of logic.

What more can a mystery addict desire than a much-loathed murder victim found aboard the luxurious Orient Express with multiple stab wounds, thirteen likely suspects, an incomparably brilliant detective in Hercule Poirot, and the most ingenious crime ever conceived?
